What is HUI?

The Hawai'i Undergraduate Initiative (HUI) is a summer leadership program providing a free 3-credit summer college course for a select group of incoming freshmen at the University of Hawai'i at Mānoa. In addition to the summer course, HUI offers engaging and insightful workshops on using university resources, exploring academics/careers and developing writing/math skills. All HUI participating scholars will be paired with a Peer Mentor who will support and assist them in their transition from high school to college.

Summer 2026 Available Courses

Each class is worth three credits and meets at least one of UH Mānoa's general education or foundation requirements for graduation.

English 100 - Composition I

Instructor: Jiyoung Lee

Introduction to the rhetorical, conceptual and stylistic demands of writing at the university level; instruction in composing processes, search strategies, and writing from sources. (FW, 3 credits)


Ethnic Studies 101 - Introduction to Ethnic Studies

Instructor: Richard Jay "RJ" Ozoa-Aglugub

Basic concepts and theories for analyzing dynamics of ethnic group experiences, particularly those represented in Hawai‘i, and their relation to colonization, immigration, problems of identity, racism, and social class. (DS, HAP 3 credits)


Education Foundations 310 - Race, Class, Gender in Popular Culture

Instructor: Aaron Machida

Education impacts individuals from all professions. To sustain occupational growth and knowledge, it is important to understand how education evolved over time. Educational progression and inquiry within American society will be explored within this course. An examination of these facets will be conducted through oral discussion, writing, and reflection. (DS, 3 credits)

Our Peer Mentors

Students will be grouped with peer mentors who support and assist them in their transition. Each peer mentor session focuses around a topic and students will engage in various activities surrounding that topic. Students will discuss transitional topics to ease their anxieties and their transition prior to the start of their first day. In addition students will debrief about each topic and have the opportunity to put these topics into action.

These peer mentors will typically be in the same field/interest as the student and will be there to guide them through the summer and academic school year. Our current PMs are listed below. You can also get to know them on our Instagram page!

Resource Workshops

Resource Workshops are designed to help students connect to some of the resources on campus to facilitate their transition to the university. These resource workshops cover a topic and discuss ways students can get involved with that resource. Examples of previous resource workshops we've held include:

  • Warrior Recreation Center Tours
  • Hamilton Library Tours
  • Manoa Career Center
  • Financial Aid
  • Learning Assistance Center
  • PAU Violence
  • Counseling and Student Development
  • Study Abroad Program
  • ... and many more!
